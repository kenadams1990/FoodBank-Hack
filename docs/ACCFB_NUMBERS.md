## 4. Quantified Problem — Per Hub Theme

Use these paragraphs verbatim (or lightly edited) in pitch deck slides. Each is anchored to at
least one verified number from §1.

### 🔭 See It Coming (Forecast / Procure Agent)

ACCFB receives **~20 inbound trucks per day** carrying volatile, donated, and seasonal supply
against a **persistent, predictable** demand from **400+ partner agencies** serving the equivalent
of **1 million meals per week**. Today, a supply gap discovered late means scrambling to purchase
coverage at spot prices — the most expensive option. Every week of earlier visibility on a
shortfall is a week in which the procurement team can source donated or discounted food instead
of paying a premium. With **$130.9M flowing through the organization** (FY2024), even a 1%
improvement in procurement timing represents **>$1.3M in potential savings or additional meals**.
*[Verify exact premium-per-lb and % of food purchased with ACCFB supply-chain team.]*

### 🏭 Production is Manufacturing (Production / Repack Agent)

ACCFB's **12,918 volunteers** contributed **93,016 hours** in FY2024 — the equivalent of
**~45 full-time staff people**. Volunteer time is the single largest non-cash input in the
warehouse. Unstaged lines, unexpected no-shows, or mis-matched task assignments burn this
input irreversibly: a volunteer hour wasted cannot be recovered. Even a **5% improvement in
volunteer utilization** recovers the equivalent of **2+ FTE** of warehouse capacity — enough to
process roughly **3 million additional pounds of food per year** (at the ~60M lbs ÷ 45 FTE
rate). *[Verify repack throughput rate and typical no-show % with ACCFB supply-chain team.]*

### 🗺️ Equity With a Truck Attached (Equity Routing Agent)

ACCFB delivers to **400+ partner agencies** across Alameda County — a county where food
insecurity is deeply concentrated in specific ZIP codes (94621/East Oakland at index 94.8,
94601 at 90.0, 94607 at 80.4). Over **45% of all food distributed is fresh produce and perishables**
— meaning every routing decision is simultaneously a logistics problem and a food-justice
decision. Distance-only routing allows perishables to sit behind shelf-stable freight and
misses agency access windows, compounding inequity for the highest-need neighborhoods.
*[Verify lbs of perishables spoiled/month and % of deliveries outside usable access windows
with ACCFB supply-chain team.]*

### 📊 Every Team Member an Analyst (Analyst Agent)

ACCFB operates a **$130.9M organization** (FY2024 revenue) with thousands of moving parts —
donor agencies, 20 inbound trucks/day, 400+ outbound partners, 12,918 volunteers —
all generating operational data. Today, the distance from a floor question
("How much produce did we send to East Oakland last month?") to a data answer is measured
in days or weeks of analyst time. TideLift's natural-language analyst agent compresses that
latency to **seconds**, putting every warehouse supervisor and partner coordinator on equal
footing with the data team. *[Quantify current analyst request turnaround time with ACCFB
operations team.]*
